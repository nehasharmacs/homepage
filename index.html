Projects

The Victim Forecast: Predicting Mass Shooting Outcomes with 50 years of Data

The Victim Forecast project develops a predictive tool to classify mass shooting incidents by risk levels using historical data from 1965 to 2019. Leveraging machine learning models such as decision trees, multinomial logistic regression, and Naive Bayes classifiers, the study identifies key factors - location, shooter demographics, and mental health status - that influence risk levels. Among the tested models, a decision tree with a quartile-based classification method and Youden's J statistic proved most effective in balancing recall and accuracy for detecting very high-risk incidents. This research provides actionable insights for policymakers and law enforcement to enhance public safety and proactive prevention measures.

Predicting Airbnb Booking Likelihood: A Data-Driven Approach

This project explores the factors influencing Airbnb booking likelihood using machine learning techniques. By analyzing property attributes, host details, and pricing data, the study employs Logistic Regression and Decision Tree models to predict whether a listing will be booked. The findings indicate that Decision Trees outperform Logistic Regression, achieving an 84% accuracy and perfect recall, making them the best choice for identifying high-likelihood bookings. These insights provide valuable recommendations for Airbnb hosts to optimize their listings and for the platform to refine its search algorithms, dynamic pricing, and marketing strategies.
